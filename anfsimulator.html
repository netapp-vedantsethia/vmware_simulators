Azure VMware Solution with Azure NetApp Files

    ANF Deployment

    Create New ANF NetApp Account
  1. Click on + Create.
  2. Enter the details:
    • Name: Demo
    • Subscription: Hybrid Cloud TME Onprem
    • Resource Group: ANFAVSVal2
    • Location: West Europe
  3. Click on Create
    4. ANF NetApp Account Demo is now created
    Create New ANF Capacity Pool
  4. Click on Demo
  5. From the Navigation pane, click on Capacity pools
  6. Click on + Add pool.
  7. Enter the details:
    • Name: DemoANFDS
    • Service Level: Ultra
    • Size (TiB): 4
    • QoS Type: Auto
  8. Click on Create
    9. ANF Capacity Pool DemoANFDS is now created
    Create New ANF Volume
  9. From the Navigation pane, click on Volumes
  10. Click on + Add volume.
  11. Enter the details:
    • Volume name: DemoANFDSVol
    • Capacity Pool: DemoANFDS
    • Quota (GiB): 4096
    • Virtual network: ANGAVSVal2
    • Delegated subnet: AVS_ANF_Subnet
    • Network features: Standard
  12. Click on Next: Protocol >
  13. Enter the details:
    • Protocol type: NFS
    • File path: DemoANFDSVol
    • Versions: NFSv3
    • Azure VMware Solution DataStore: Yes
  14. Review the Export policy and click Next: Tags >
  15. Enter the required key-value pair details and click on Review + Create
  16. Click on Create
    17. ANF Volume DemoANFDSVol is now created. Click on demoanfdsvol to view details.

    AVS Deployment

    Create New AVS Private Cloud
  17. On the AVS screen, click on + Create.
  18. Click on Next: Basics >.
  19. Enter the details on the Basics tab:
    • Subscription: Hybrid Cloud TME Onprem
    • Resource Group: ANFAVSVal2
    • Resource Name: ANFDataClus
    • Location: West Europe
    • Size: AV36
    • Number of hosts: 3
    • Address block: 172.30.156.0/22
  20. Click on Next: Tags >
  21. Enter the required key-value pair details and click on Review + Create
  22. Click on Create
    23. AVS Private Cloud ANFDataClus is now created. Click on ANFDataClus to view details.

    Mount ANF Volume on AVS

  23. From the AVS Navigation pane, click on Storage (preview)
  24. Selectâ€¯+ Connect Azure NetApp Files volume
  25. Enter the details:
    • Subscription: Hybrid Cloud TME Onprem
    • NetApp account: Demo
    • Capacity pool: DemoANFDS
    • Volume: DemoANFDSVol
    • Client cluster: Cluster-1
    • Datastore name: DemoANFDSVol
  26. Click Connect
    27. DataStore DemoANFDSVol is mounted successfully.

    Migrate a VM on AVS to ANF DataStore

  27. From the AVS Navigation pane, click on Identity to get vCenter server credentials.
  28. Navigate to Virtual Machines in Microsoft Azure portal
  29. Click on ANFAVFVal2JH virtual machine jumphost.
  30. Click on Connect and select RDP from the dropdown menu.
  31. Open the browser window inside the VM and enter the vCenter server URL in the URL search bar
  32. Click on LAUNCH VSPHERE CLIENT (HTML5)
  33. Enter the vCenter credentials and click on Login
  34. Navigate to the Storage tab and verify if DemoANFDSVol ANF volume is mounted as a DataStore
  35. Navigate back to the VM tab and right click on UbuntuSrv01 VM
  36. Click on Migrate
  37. Select the Change storage only option and click on Next
  38. Under VM Storage Policy, select DataStore Default
  39. Select DemoANFDSVol and click on Next
  40. Review the changes and click on Finish
  41. Monitor the migration activity in the Recent Tasks section
  42. Navigate to the Storage tab once completed
  43. Click on VMs section under DemoANFDSVol
    44. The VM UbuntuSrv01 can be seen sitting on the ANF volume added as a DataStore on AVS. This completes the section on migrating a VM to ANF DataStore on AVS

    Resize ANF volume

    Resize Capacity Pool
  44. Click on Demo NetApp Account in the ANF section of Microsoft Azure
  45. Navigate to Capacity Pools
  46. Click on the three dots next to DemoANFDS and click on Resize
  47. Change the size from 4 TiB to 8 TiB
  48. Click on OK. The capacity pool has been resized to 8 TiB

    49. Resize Volume
  49. Click on Volumes
  50. Click on the three dots next to DemoANFDSVol and click on Resize
  51. Change Quota from 4096 GiB to 8192 GiB
  52. Click on OK. The volume has been resized to 8192 GiB

    53. Verification on AVS
  53. Navigate back to the VM from where we accessed the vCenter
  54. Navigate to the Storage tab and right-click on DemoANFDSVol
  55. Click on Refresh Capacity Information
    56. The Storage capacity can now be seen to be updated from 4 TiB to 8 Tib. This concludes the section on resizing the ANF volume size

    Disconnect and Delete ANF volume

    Disconnect ANF volume from AVS
  56. On the Azure VMware Solutions section in Microsoft Azure, click on ANFDataClus
  57. Navigate to Storage (preview)
  58. Click on the three dots next to DemoANFDSVol and click on Disconnect
  59. Click on OK. The volume has been disconnected from AVS

    60. Delete the ANF volume
  60. Navigate to the ANF volume
  61. On the ANF DemoANFDSVol volume page, click on Delete
  62. Type the name demoanfdsvol
  63. Click on Delete. The volume has now been deleted

    64. Delete the ANF Capacity Pool
  64. Navigate to the Capacity pool
  65. Click on the three dots next to DemoANFDS and click on Delete
  66. Click on Yes. The capacity pool has now been deleted

    67. Delete the ANF NetApp account
  67. Navigate to the Overview
  68. Click on Delete
  69. Click on Yes. The NetApp account has now been deleted
    70. This concludes the section on disconnecting and deleting ANF volume. The lab is completed. Thank you.
